PARTSMART AI TUTORIAL

Complete guide to transforming your scrap into engineered projects

What is PartSmart AI?

PartSmart AI is a multi-modal engineering assistant that uses Google Gemini AI to analyze your spare parts, scrap materials, and surplus components — then generates complete, buildable project designs with 3D renders, cut lists, and step-by-step assembly instructions.

Supported project types: Furniture, 3D Printers, CNC Machines, Robotics, and General Engineering.

1 Getting Started

1.1 — First Launch & Authentication

  1. Open PartSmart AI. On first launch, you'll see the AI Connection screen.
  2. Choose your authentication method:
    • Sign in with Google (Recommended) — Uses your Google account's Gemini quota at no extra cost. Tap the Google button, approve the sign-in, and you're connected.
    • API Key — If you have a Google AI Studio API key, enter it manually. Get one free at aistudio.google.com/apikey.
  3. Once connected, you'll land on the Studio tab — your main workspace.

1.2 — The Three Tabs

Studio

Your main design workspace. Upload photos of parts or type descriptions, then generate AI project designs.

Warehouse

Your master inventory. View all cataloged parts with photos, dimensions, and quantities. Import/Export backups.

Gallery

Your completed project showcase. Browse past designs, view details, and share results.

2 Building Your Inventory

2.1 — Adding Parts by Photo

  1. In the Studio tab, tap the camera icon to photograph your parts.
  2. For best results:
    • Lay parts on a flat, contrasting surface (dark background for light parts, vice versa)
    • Include a ruler or coin for scale reference
    • Photograph multiple similar parts together in one shot
    • Use good lighting — avoid heavy shadows
  3. The AI will automatically identify each part, estimate dimensions, and add them to your Warehouse inventory.
  4. Review the detected parts and edit any incorrect dimensions or labels by tapping on a part.

2.2 — Adding Parts by Text

  1. In the Studio tab, tap the text icon (keyboard symbol).
  2. Type or paste a description of your parts. Examples:
    • "4x aluminum extrusion 2040, 500mm long"
    • "12x M5 T-nuts, 8x corner brackets, 24x M5x10 bolts"
    • Paste from a spreadsheet, product page, or parts list
  3. The AI will parse your text into structured inventory items with estimated dimensions.

2.3 — Smart BOM Assistant

Found a product online that has parts you want? The Smart BOM Assistant can extract structured parts data from URLs.

  1. Go to the Warehouse tab.
  2. Tap Smart BOM Assistant.
  3. Paste a product URL (e.g., Amazon, Home Depot, AliExpress) or describe what you need.
  4. The AI will search the web, identify individual parts in the kit, and show you a structured list.
  5. Review the results, adjust quantities, and confirm to add them to your inventory.

2.4 — Editing Parts

Tap any part in the Warehouse to open the Inventory Editor. You can adjust length, width, height, unit type, material, and quantity. Use the crop tool to isolate a specific part from a group photo.

2.5 — Calibration Card

For more accurate AI dimension estimates, print the built-in calibration card (accessible from the Warehouse). Place it next to your parts when photographing — the AI uses the reference scale to improve accuracy.

3 Generating a Project Design

3.1 — Configure Your Build

  1. In the Studio tab, you'll see three configuration options above the "Synthesize Designs" button:
  2. Module — Select your engineering domain:
    • Furniture — Tables, shelves, desks, cabinets
    • 3D Printer — Custom printer frames, extruders, enclosures
    • CNC Machine — Router tables, laser cutters, plasma tables
    • Robotics — Arms, rovers, automation rigs
    • General — Any other project type
  3. Skill Level — Be honest about your abilities:
    • Beginner — Hand tools only. Simple joinery, pre-cut materials.
    • Maker — Power tools available. Moderate joinery, some custom fabrication.
    • Engineer — Full workshop. Complex CAD-level precision, welding, machining.
  4. Build Concept — Describe what you want to build. Be specific:
    • Good: "Modern L-shaped standing desk with monitor shelf"
    • Good: "CoreXY 3D printer with 300mm build volume"
    • Bad: "Something cool"

3.2 — The 8-Step Engineering Pipeline

When you tap Synthesize Designs, PartSmart AI runs an 8-step pipeline powered by Gemini 3 Pro and Flash Lite:

  1. Catalog — Organize all inventory parts into categories
  2. Group — Cluster parts by structural role (frame, fasteners, panels)
  3. Layout — Arrange parts in 3D space to match your concept
  4. Map — Assign part IDs to positions in the design
  5. Draft — Generate initial design proposals
  6. Render — Create lifelike 3D visualizations
  7. Audit — Gemini Pro performs structural feasibility analysis
  8. Finalize — Generate exploded views, cut lists, and assembly data

You'll see an animated console showing progress through each step. The full pipeline typically takes 30-90 seconds depending on inventory size.

3.3 — Reviewing Your Designs

After synthesis completes, you'll see 3 design proposals. Each shows a 3D render, project name, description, and estimated material savings. Tap any card to open the full Detail View.

4 The Detail View

The Detail View has 4 tabs:

Overview

Project summary, difficulty rating, estimated build time, required tools list, and material conservation score. Includes buttons to launch the Assembly Wizard and Structural Audit.

Parts

Complete list of inventory items used in this design, with photos, quantities, and dimensions. See exactly which parts go where.

Blueprints

Multi-angle views (front 3D, top 2D, side 2D, back 3D) and exploded assembly diagrams. Includes the complete Bill of Materials (BOM) table with cut dimensions.

Logic

The raw Gemini Pro engineering log — see exactly how the AI reasoned through structural decisions, material choices, and safety factors. Great for learning engineering principles.

5 Assembly Wizard

  1. From the Detail View Overview tab, tap Assembly Wizard.
  2. The wizard generates a unique technical diagram for each assembly step.
  3. Navigate between steps using the arrows or progress bar.
  4. Each step shows:
    • A generated technical diagram of the step
    • Written instructions with part references
    • Required parts and tools for that specific step
  5. The wizard pre-fetches the next step's image for smooth navigation.

6 Tips & Best Practices

Photography Tips

Use natural daylight. Lay parts on a solid-color surface. Group similar parts together. Include something for scale (ruler, coin). Avoid cluttered backgrounds.

Better Build Concepts

Be specific about size, style, and function. Mention constraints ("must fit in a 2ft x 3ft corner"). Describe the aesthetic ("industrial", "minimalist", "mid-century modern"). Mention what it needs to hold or support.

Inventory Management

Keep your Warehouse organized. Use the Import/Export feature to back up your inventory regularly. The more accurate your part dimensions, the better the AI designs will be.

Structural Library

Need standard components? The Structural Library includes pre-configured linear rails, stepper motors, aluminum extrusions, brackets, and fasteners — ready to add to your Warehouse instantly.

Safety Disclaimer

All designs are AI-generated. Always verify structural integrity with a qualified engineer before building load-bearing or safety-critical projects. PartSmart AI is a design aid, not a replacement for professional engineering review.

7 Troubleshooting

"AI Connection Failed"

Go to Settings (gear icon) → AI Connection. Test your connection. If using Google Sign-In, try signing out and back in. If using an API key, verify it's valid at aistudio.google.com.

"Design generation is slow"

The 8-step pipeline is compute-intensive. Larger inventories take longer. Generation typically takes 30-90 seconds. Ensure you have a stable internet connection.

"Parts weren't identified correctly"

Tap the misidentified part in the Warehouse to manually edit its name, dimensions, and material type. Better photos with scale references improve AI accuracy significantly.

"Rate limited / Quota exceeded"

The free Gemini tier has daily usage limits. Wait a few hours and try again, or upgrade to a paid Gemini plan for higher quotas.
